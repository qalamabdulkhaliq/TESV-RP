# Frostfall Roleplay — Server Rules

**Setting:** 4E 191, Skyrim. Ten years before the events of The Elder Scrolls V.
**Access:** Whitelisted. Application required before joining.

---

## 1. Core Principles

- This is a narrative roleplay server. Systems exist to support the story, not replace it.
- In-character consequences handle most violations. Staff intervene when IC cannot.
- You are responsible for knowing these rules. Ignorance is not a defense.
- Staff have final say. Appeals go through the ticket system, not public channels.

---

## 2. Characters

### 2.1 Character Creation
- Original characters only. No character may use the name of a canonical TES character.
- Characters must be lore-consistent. Races, backgrounds, and abilities follow TES lore.
- One character per player at launch.
- Character names must be appropriate for the setting. No joke names, modern names, or names from other franchises.

### 2.2 Character Conduct
- **Metagaming is prohibited.** Information your character does not have cannot influence your character's actions. OOC knowledge stays OOC.
- **Powergaming is prohibited.** You cannot force outcomes on another player's character without their consent, except within the combat and downed rules below.
- **Godmodding is prohibited.** Your character is not invincible, all-knowing, or immune to consequence.
- Stay in character in IC spaces. OOC text in IC channels must use double brackets: `(( like this ))`.

### 2.3 Character Death
- Permanent character death requires mutual OOC consent from both parties, except in the following cases:
  - A character is executed by a Jarl following IC judicial process
  - A character is killed during a sanctioned KOID engagement with no staff dispute filed within 24 hours
- Staff do not arbitrate character death disputes arising from roleplay both parties consented to.

---

## 3. Combat

### 3.1 Kill on Sight (KOS)
- Unprovoked killing of another player is not permitted.
- You must have an established, documented IC reason before initiating lethal combat.
- Exceptions are listed under KOID below.

### 3.2 Kill on ID (KOID)
Certain faction pairs have standing mutual lethal-force authorization. No prior IC interaction required.

| Faction A | Faction B |
|-----------|-----------|
| Thalmor | Stormcloak Underground |
| Imperial Garrison | Stormcloak Underground |
| Hold Guards | Players with bounty at or above 1,000 Septims in that Hold |

- KOID is **symmetric**. Both sides may engage.
- KOID does not authorize killing civilians caught nearby. Collateral kills require IC justification.
- Joining a KOID faction is an explicit acknowledgment that you may be killed on sight by the opposing faction.

### 3.3 Downed State
When a fight ends, the loser is **Downed**, not dead.

- A downed character is incapacitated. They cannot act until released or until the scene concludes.
- The victor may loot a maximum of **500 Septims** and **3 items**.
- Full looting is prohibited. Taking everything is an exploitable offense.
- The victor may leave, hold the downed character captive, or negotiate ransom.
- Downed characters may not respawn or disconnect to avoid RP consequences. Log out only if there is a genuine emergency. Reconnecting within 30 minutes of a combat log-out is an offense.

### 3.4 No Value For Life (NVFL)
If a character is Downed or Captured:
- They may not initiate or join hostilities for the next **24 IRL hours**.
- They may defend themselves if attacked during that period.
- NVFL is tracked mechanically. The system enforces it.

### 3.5 Captivity
- A captive character must be held no longer than **24 IRL hours**.
- After 24 hours, restraints despawn automatically. The captive is free regardless of RP state.
- Captors must make a genuine RP effort to resolve the scene before the timer expires: ransom, imprisonment, or negotiated release.
- Captivity cannot be used to remove a player from the game indefinitely.

## 4. Justice System

### 4.1 Bounties
- Bounties are issued by Hold Guards (player-controlled) for witnessed crimes.
- Bounties are per-hold. A bounty in Whiterun does not apply in Riften.
- Bounties at or above 1,000 Septims in a Hold authorize Hold Guards to kill on sight.

### 4.2 Arrest and Imprisonment
- Arrested characters must stand before the Hold's Jarl for sentencing.
- Sentencing options: fine, labor, banishment, or worse.
- The Jarl's decision is final IC. It may be appealed OOC only on grounds of staff misconduct, not because you disagree with the IC outcome.
- Repeat offenders build a record with that Jarl. Records are persistent.

### 4.3 Banishment
- A banished character may not enter the banishing Hold.
- Mechanical enforcement applies where available.
- Repeatedly entering a Hold you have been banished from is an OOC offense.

---

## 5. Faction Rules

### 5.1 Joining Factions
- Joining a faction is an IC process. You must RP your way in.
- Factions have a BBB document (Benefits, Burdens, Bylaws). Read it before applying. Ignorance of your faction's bylaws is not an excuse.
- Faction leadership approves membership. Staff do not intervene in membership decisions.

### 5.2 Faction Conduct
- Each faction's Bylaws define acceptable conduct within that faction.
- Bylaws violations are handled IC first. The Thalmor corrects the College. The Jarl corrects the guard.
- Repeated bylaw violations that cannot be handled IC may be escalated to staff.

### 5.3 Omitted Factions
- The Dark Brotherhood does not exist on this server as a recognized faction.
- The Stormcloaks do not exist as an organized army at launch. Ulfric Stormcloak is a prisoner. Players may roleplay resistance politics, but there is no formal faction structure, no BBB document, and no mechanical recognition until players build it IC.

## 6. Leadership

### 6.1 The Leadership Covenant
Players in key faction and Hold leadership roles have additional responsibilities.

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Tenure | Leaders hold their role until they retire or pass it on IC |
| Inactivity | 2 weeks with no IC roleplay results in the role being shelved |
| Succession | Rank passes IC to a designated or emergent successor |
| Return | Players may return to their character, not automatically to their role |

### 6.2 Leadership Applications
- Leadership applications require a demonstrated understanding of the relevant BBB document.
- Major seats (Jarl, faction head) require an OOC interview.
- Staff seed leadership at launch. Subsequent leaders emerge through IC process.

---

## 7. OOC Conduct

### 7.1 Harassment
- Harassment of any kind is prohibited. This includes continued unwanted contact after a player has asked to stop.
- Harassment that begins IC and continues OOC is treated as an OOC violation.

### 7.2 Discrimination
- Slurs, hate speech, and real-world discrimination have no place in this community, IC or OOC.
- Lore-consistent racial tensions between in-game races (Dunmer and Argonians, Nords and elves) are part of the setting. These must stay IC and must not be used as cover for real-world discrimination.

### 7.3 Exploits
- Exploiting game mechanics for advantage is prohibited.
- Finding an exploit and not reporting it is treated the same as using it.
- Report exploits via the `#bug-reports` channel or a staff ticket.

### 7.4 Disruption
- Intentional disruption of other players' roleplay with no IC justification is prohibited.
- Spamming, griefing, or acting in ways designed to make the experience worse for others will result in removal.

### 7.5 Alts
- Alt accounts require staff approval.
- Using an alt to bypass a ban or punishment is an immediate permanent ban of all accounts.

---

## 8. Staff

- Staff play characters. Their character's IC actions are IC. Staff decisions about the server are OOC.
- Disagreements with an IC decision made by a staff character are IC matters. Handle them IC.
- Disagreements with an OOC staff decision go through the ticket system.
- Do not contact staff members in DMs about server matters. Use tickets.
- Staff are not available 24/7. Response times vary.

---

## 9. Whitelist and Applications

- The server is whitelisted. Access requires an approved application.
- General applications require a character name, race, background, and faction interest.
- Tailored applications are required for certain roles (see `#how-to-apply`).
- Accepted characters may be denied specific faction access if their concept conflicts with that faction's BBB document.
- Staff may revoke whitelist access for repeated or severe rule violations.

---

## 10. Enforcement

| Severity | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Minor first offense | Warning via ticket |
| Minor repeat offense | Temporary ban (duration varies) |
| Moderate offense | Temporary ban, character consequence |
| Severe offense | Permanent ban |
| Exploit use | Immediate temporary ban, review |
| Harassment | Immediate temporary or permanent ban |
| Ban evasion | Immediate permanent ban, all accounts |

- All enforcement decisions are logged.
- Appeals are submitted via ticket, not in public channels.
- Appeal decisions are final.
